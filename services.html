Services

Short Circuit Analysis

An electrical short circuit occurs when two or more energized parts of an electrical equipment form un unintended contact, resulting in an immediate surge of electrical current. This could create dangerous conditions that threaten human safety and compromise equipment integrity.

In order to identify these potentially hazardous conditions and prevent electrical equipment damage within a facility, a customer can proactively request a Short Circuit Analysis.

Arc Flash Hazard Analysis

An arc flash hazard analysis is required to determine the incident energy levels and arc flash boundaries at each system bus.

Calculated values are used to assess the arc flash hazard risk at equipment included, apply arc flash hazard label in the field, and help the selection of appropriate personal protective equipment in compliance with NFPA 70E.

Learn More

Protective Device Coordination

A protective device coordination study selects the appropriate settings for all overcurrent protective devices in the system to verify that, in the event of a fault, the device closest to the fault operates to clear the fault.

This minimizes downtime for the system and ensures that only fault equipment is taken offline.

Learn More

Equipment Evaluation Report

Based on the short circuit analysis, an equipment evaluation report will be generated. Instances where the calculated fault current is greater than the equipment ratings will be noted as “Failed” and highlighted in yellow in the equipment evaluation report as part of the study.
